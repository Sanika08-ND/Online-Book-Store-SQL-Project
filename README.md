# Online-Book-Store-SQL-Project

<h2><b>Project Overview</b></h2>

<p>This project demonstrates an Online Book Store database system implemented using SQL.
It includes database design and solutions for basic and advanced SQL queries to analyze book sales, customer behavior, and inventory.The Online Book Store Database simulates a real-world bookstore system where customers can browse books, place orders, and manage transactions.</p>

<h4>This project focuses on:</h4>
<ul>
<li>Designing relational tables</li>
<li>Writing SQL queries for data retrieval</li>
<li>Solving basic to advanced database problems</li>
<li>Understanding relationships between entities such as books, customers, and orders</li></ul>

<h2>Importing Tables</h2>
<ul><li>customers</li>
<li>books</li>
<li>orders</li></ul>

<h2>SQL Queries Solved</h2>
<h4> Basic Level Queries</h4>
<ul>
<li>Retrieve all books in the "Fiction" genre</li>

<li>Find books published after the year 1950</li>

<li>List all customers from Canada</li>

<li>Show orders placed in November 2023</li>

<li>Retrieve the total stock of books available</li>

<li>Find the details of the most expensive book</li>

<li>Show all customers who ordered more than 1 quantity of a book</li>

<li>Retrieve all orders where the total amount exceeds $20</li>

<li>List all genres available in the Books table</li>

<li>Find the book with the lowest stock</li></ul>

<h4>Advanced Level Queries</h4>
<ul>
<li>Retrieve the total number of books sold for each genre</li>

<li>Find the average price of books in the "Fantasy" genre</li>

<li>List customers who have placed at least 2 orders</li>

<li>Find the most frequently ordered book</li>

<li>Show the top 3 most expensive books in the Fantasy genre</li>

<li>Retrieve the total quantity of books sold by each author</li>

<li>List the cities where customers who spent over $30 are located</li>

<li>Find the customer who spent the most on orders</li>

<li>Calculate the stock remaining after fulfilling all orders</li>
</ul>
